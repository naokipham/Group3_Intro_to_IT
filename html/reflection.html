Overall Group Reflection

Over the course of this semester, our team has gained valuable insights into both the positive and challenging aspects of teamwork in the IT field.

What Worked Well

Our team fully formed around the middle of the semester. Once assembled, we made concerted efforts to get along and familiarize ourselves with each other. This resulted in a cooperative atmosphere that was beneficial for both our productivity and the mood of individuals. Our team's diverse backgrounds have enriched our capability to generate ideas from various perspectives. Many of us are highly passionate about IT and share the goal of earning a high grade in the course. This common objective has laid the foundation for effective teamwork. We also prioritized early meetings to discuss task assignments and project details, which has helped us stay on schedule and reduce the risk of missing deadlines.

Areas for Improvement

Despite the teamwork parts we relatively succeed on, there are areas that need attention. Our primary mode of communication has been online chat apps, notably Messenger, without enough in-person meetings. We’ve found that the depth of engagement differs significantly between virtual and face-to-face interactions, affecting overall performance. To remedy this, we can plan to schedule more in-person sessions in the future. In terms of time management, we recognized that although we aim for high grades, achieving this goal requires careful planning. We observed that stress levels increase as deadlines approach closer, often leading to rushed work and lower quality. To improve, we plan to familiarize ourselves with time management systems and raise awareness about the pitfalls of procrastination.

Surprising Challenges

Along the way, we've encountered a few surprises. For instance, some of us were unprepared for the complexities of web development techniques. Coding in real-life environment comes with its set of challenges due to the ever-changing external variables and complexity of software in the real-world development environment. This experience has emphasized the need for our continuous learning. We also faced challenges with GitHub. While we were excited about its practical usage, the steep learning curve has generated troublesome difficulties to us. We realized that to fully utilize GitHub, we would need to invest more time to try and learn than initially anticipated.

Lessons on Groups

Through trials and errors, we've learned that effective teamwork is more complex than initially thought. Firstly, a group is a dynamic entity, with members' circumstances and attitudes subject to change. We need to adapt our strategies to accommodate new situation and enable work-around and makeshifts. Secondly, diversity among team members offers both opportunities and challenges. We must learn how to manage these differences effectively, seeking common ground while respecting individual preferences. Lastly, consistent communication is crucial for successful teamwork. The absence or “ghosting” of any member from active participation can be detrimental and disappointing. We should keep this in mind when forming new teams in the future.
